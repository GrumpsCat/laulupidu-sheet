Zero Trust Architecture – A cybersecurity model that assumes no user or device is inherently trusted, even if they are inside the network. Every access request must be continuously verified using identity, context, and device health.

The Akkadian Empire – The world’s first known empire (c. 2334–2154 BCE), created by Sargon of Akkad in Mesopotamia. It unified diverse city-states under one rule and pioneered imperial administration, standing armies, and a shared Semitic language.

Multiple Sequence Alignment (MSA) – A core bioinformatics method that aligns three or more biological sequences (DNA, RNA, or protein) to identify conserved regions. These alignments reveal evolutionary relationships, functional domains, and mutation hotspots.

Hákarl – A traditional Icelandic food made from fermented Greenland shark. The shark’s flesh is naturally toxic when fresh and becomes edible only after months of fermentation and drying, producing a pungent ammonia-rich flavor considered an acquired taste.
