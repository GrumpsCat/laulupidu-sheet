Sleep Sheet

This study examined whether parental reports of sleep problems in children with autism spectrum disorder (ASD) correspond to objective physiological sleep differences and whether these sleep patterns relate to daytime behavior. Fifty-eight children (ages 4–10) completed two nights of simultaneous actigraphy and polysomnography (PSG) along with standardized sleep and behavioral assessments. ASD “poor sleepers,” as rated by parents, showed significantly longer sleep latency and greater sleep fragmentation, along with reduced sleep efficiency and increased wake after sleep onset on actigraphy. These children also exhibited higher levels of inattention, hyperactivity, and repetitive/compulsive behaviors, with fragmentation correlating strongly with ritualistic and restrictive behaviors. Results demonstrate that parental sleep concerns reliably map onto quantifiable sleep phenotypes, positioning PSG and actigraphy as complementary methods for defining sleep dysfunction and guiding targeted interventions.

Reference: Goldman, S. E., Surdyka, K., Cuevas, R., Adkins, K., Wang, L., & Malow, B. A. (2009). Defining the sleep phenotype in children with autism. Developmental Neuropsychology, 34(5), 560–573.

Polysomnography (PSG) – A comprehensive overnight sleep study measuring EEG, breathing, movement, oxygen saturation, and sleep stages.

Actigraphy – A wrist-worn motion sensor that estimates sleep and wake states based on movement patterns across the night.

Sleep Latency – The amount of time it takes to fall asleep after lights out; prolonged latency indicates difficulty initiating sleep.

Sleep Efficiency – The percentage of time in bed actually spent asleep, used as a broad measure of sleep quality.

Wake After Sleep Onset (WASO) – The total duration of awakenings that occur after initially falling asleep, reflecting sleep continuity.

Fragmentation Index – An actigraphy-derived measurement of nighttime restlessness capturing movement-related microarousals.

Repetitive Behavior Scale–Revised (RBS-R) – A standardized measure of stereotyped, compulsive, ritualistic, and restrictive behaviors in ASD.

Child Behavior Checklist (CBCL) – A parent-report tool assessing daytime emotional and behavioral problems such as inattention and hyperactivity.

Children’s Sleep Habits Questionnaire (CSHQ) – A validated survey assessing insomnia, parasomnias, sleep anxiety, and night wakings.

Sleep Phenotype – A measurable profile of sleep characteristics that defines a subgroup (e.g., ASD poor sleepers vs. good sleepers).

Parental Concerns Questionnaire (PCQ) – A questionnaire used to classify children as “good” or “poor” sleepers based on parental concern severity.
