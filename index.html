Sleep Sheet

Glymphatic Clearance – A waste-removal system in the central nervous system that uses perivascular pathways to clear metabolic byproducts during sleep, facilitated by glial cells and cerebrospinal fluid flow.

Nigra and α-Synuclein – Refers to the substantia nigra, a midbrain structure involved in dopamine production, and α-synuclein, a presynaptic neuronal protein whose misfolding and aggregation are associated with several neurodegenerative diseases.

Brainstem Autonomy – The capacity of brainstem nuclei to independently regulate essential physiological functions such as respiration, heart rate, arousal, reflexes, and sleep–wake transitions.

Dream-Like Cognition – A mode of thought characterized by vivid imagery, associative reasoning, and reduced logical constraint, resembling the mental activity experienced during REM sleep.

REM Atonia – A paralysis-like state occurring during rapid eye movement (REM) sleep in which skeletal muscles become temporarily inactive to prevent the physical acting out of dreams.

Arousal Systems (Reticular Activating System) – A network of brainstem structures that regulate wakefulness, attention, and overall cortical activation through widespread projections to the cerebral cortex.

Cholinergic Modulation – Neural signaling involving the neurotransmitter acetylcholine, which influences attention, learning, REM sleep generation, and arousal through both cortical and subcortical pathways.

Dopaminergic Signaling – Communication between neurons using the neurotransmitter dopamine, essential for motor control, reward processing, motivational behavior, and several cognitive functions.

Autonomic Regulation – Control of involuntary physiological processes such as heart rate, digestion, respiration, and vascular tone through the sympathetic and parasympathetic branches of the autonomic nervous system.

Synaptic Homeostasis – The maintenance of stable synaptic strength across neural networks, often associated with sleep-related processes that rebalance synaptic activity after waking experience.

Cortical Arousal – The activation level of the cerebral cortex, influencing alertness, sensory processing, cognitive readiness, and responsiveness to environmental stimuli.

Thalamocortical Rhythm – Oscillatory activity generated through interactions between the thalamus and cortex, underlying consciousness, sensory gating, sleep stages, and attentional states.

Sleep Architecture – The structure and progression of sleep stages, including non-REM and REM sleep cycles, typically represented through a hypnogram across the night.

REM–NREM Cycling – The alternation between REM sleep and non-REM sleep stages during a typical sleep period, regulated by coordinated activity in brainstem and hypothalamic circuits.

Neuroinflammation – Immune activation within the central nervous system involving microglia, astrocytes, and cytokines, often occurring in response to injury, infection, or neurodegenerative processes.

Circadian Entrainment – The synchronization of the body's internal biological clock to external environmental cues such as light, temperature, or social activity.

Basal Ganglia Circuitry – Interconnected nuclei including the striatum, globus pallidus, and substantia nigra that coordinate movement, action selection, and procedural learning.

Locus Coeruleus Activity – Noradrenergic signaling originating from the locus coeruleus, influential in arousal, stress responses, attention, and sleep–wake regulation.

Hypothalamic Sleep Switch – A set of hypothalamic neurons, including the ventrolateral preoptic nucleus (VLPO), that act as a regulatory mechanism controlling the transition between sleep and wakefulness.
